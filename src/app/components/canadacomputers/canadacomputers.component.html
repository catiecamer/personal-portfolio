Canada Computers Design Case Study

/01
Background

About the Project

The following is a UX case study for the Canada Computers website completed by five students at General Assembly. Canada Computers is a retailer of computers, components, and electronics with a mission to provide the best products at the best value. With a timeline of two weeks, we directed our focus to the checkout process, which most users identified as pain points in early rounds of task analysis. The website was analyzed on both, a mobile and desktop platform.

User Interviews

Canada Computers consumer: 53%
Of the 15 users that were interviewed, 8 had shopped at Canada Computers in the past
Computer builder: 47%
Of the 8 that had shopped at Canada Computers, 7 had shopped for the purpose of building or fixing their computers

/04
Persona

The Computer Builder

"I am searching for components that meet specific performance criteria. The computer component is my priority and I am willing to go the extra mile, including traveling, to obtain the part I desire."
Motivations
  • I want to choose my specific setup
  • I feel confident because I know what went into my computer and can easily fix/update it
  • It’s the best price to performance ratio
Pain Points
  • Too much effort and time spent researching
  • No assistance causes doubt
  • Comparing all aspects of a product involves too many resources
