THAT Toronto Studio

About the project

In a three-week UX design project for THAT Toronto Studio, I was tasked with suggesting UI and UX improvements for their current desktop and mobile websites based on user feedback. The focus was on enhancing the landing page and booking process, which were identified as crucial elements in improving the overall user experience.

What is THAT Toronto Studio and who are the users?

THAT Toronto Studio was founded in 2016 as a top-rated space for photographers and videographers. It has evolved to also host events like parties and corporate meetings. According to THAT Toronto Studio, 80% of website users book for production, while 20% book for events.

Business needs

THAT Toronto Studio's business goals are to generate revenue and increase revenue growth year after year. The studio plans to do the following:

Maintain strong industry reputation

Provide high-quality services and receive positive reviews/referrals

How might we reduce information overload on the landing page?
Users of the website want to compare studios easily, but there is too much information on the website, preventing decision-making. To address this issue, the website should organize the information in a way that users can easily access it, but also have the choice to opt-out or ignore it if they want to focus on essential facts.

How might we make the booking process more intuitive & trustworthy?
Removal of Instructions
Having instructions for a booking process can harm the user experience by causing cognitive overload, disrupting the natural flow, and implying a lack of trust in users' abilities. Instead, I focused on ensuring that the step-by-step process is intuitive by adding primary & secondary buttons and drop-downs for less important information to ensure a smoother and more engaging experience.
