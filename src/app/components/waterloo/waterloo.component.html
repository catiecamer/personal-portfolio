03 Journey Map
In order to understand the prospective student’s pain points, motivations, and behaviours as well as identify opportunities to improve the user experience, we created a journey map based on the responses and emotions discovered in interviews.
For the remainder of the study, we concentrated on investigating and addressing the user's frustrations related to the "Program Exploration" and "How to Apply”. These phases caused the most negative emotions in the journey map.

07 Iterations:
‍Find fundamental info faster
Upon reviewing the pain points faced by students when exploring programs and applying to them, we recognized the importance of providing the required information quickly. Therefore, we focused on promptly presenting a program list, which was a top priority for prospective students based on our interviews.

Side navigation menu = too much time to navigate
Prospective students faced difficulties with the desktop sidebar due to the overwhelming amount of diverse information and unclear hierarchy of items. As a solution, a secondary navigation system was introduced, which users found to be a quicker and more efficient way to navigate the information.
08 Iterations:
How to apply: Step breakdown
Based on user interviews, it's clear that many prospective students find the university application process complicated and daunting. As a result, they look for straightforward steps to guide them through the process. A concise and straightforward application process is crucial because it boosts students' confidence and control, ultimately increasing their chances of submitting an application.
